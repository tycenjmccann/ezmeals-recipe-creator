# Black Pepper Chicken

**Description:** Crispy chicken tossed in a homemade black pepper sauce with sautéed bell pepper, celery, and onion, served on a bed of rice. Flavorful, comforting, and ready in about 30 minutes.

**Servings:** 6
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 15 minutes
**Cuisine:** Chinese

---

## Ingredients

### Chicken & Marinade
- 1 ½ lbs boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-size pieces (680 grams)
- 2 Tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
- 2 teaspoons oyster sauce
- 1 ½ teaspoons fresh cracked black pepper
- 1 teaspoon salt
- ½ cup cornstarch, for coating (65 grams)

### Sauce
- 1 ½ Tablespoons oyster sauce
- 3 Tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 ½ cups low-sodium chicken stock (360ml)
- 3–4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 ½ Tablespoons freshly grated ginger
- 1 ½ teaspoons fresh cracked black pepper
- 3 Tablespoons granulated sugar
- 1 ½ teaspoons cornstarch

### Stir-Fry & Serving
- ⅓ cup vegetable oil (80ml), plus more if needed
- 1 medium white onion, chopped into bite-size pieces
- 2 ribs celery, chopped
- 1 large red bell pepper, chopped into bite-size pieces
- 5–6 cups hot cooked white rice (850–1000 grams)

---

## Instructions

1. **Marinate Chicken:** Add chicken pieces to a bowl with **2 Tablespoons soy sauce, 2 teaspoons oyster sauce, 1 ½ teaspoons black pepper, and 1 teaspoon salt**. Toss to coat and set aside for at least 10 minutes (up to 30 minutes). Place **½ cup cornstarch** in a separate large bowl or resealable plastic bag and set aside.

2. **Make Sauce:** In a medium bowl, whisk together **1 ½ Tablespoons oyster sauce, 3 Tablespoons soy sauce, 1 ½ cups chicken stock, minced garlic, grated ginger, 1 ½ teaspoons black pepper, 3 Tablespoons sugar, and 1 ½ teaspoons cornstarch** until the cornstarch is fully dissolved. Set aside.

3. **Coat Chicken:** Remove chicken pieces from the marinade (discard excess marinade liquid) and add to the bowl or bag with cornstarch. Toss well to coat evenly. Shake off excess cornstarch before frying.

4. **Sauté Chicken:** Heat **⅓ cup vegetable oil** in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is shimmering, add **half** the chicken pieces, spacing them out so they aren't crowded. Cook without moving for 2–3 minutes until golden on the bottom, then flip and cook another 2–3 minutes until golden, crisp, and cooked through (internal temp 165°F/74°C). Remove to a plate lined with paper towels. Repeat with remaining chicken, adding a splash more oil if the pan looks dry.

   > **⚠️ Tip:** Don't overcrowd the pan — this is the key to crispy (not steamed) chicken. Work in two batches.

5. **Sauté Veggies:** If the pan has excess oil, pour most of it out, leaving about 1 Tablespoon. Add **onion, celery, and bell pepper**. Cook over medium-high heat, tossing occasionally, for 2–3 minutes until tender-crisp.

6. **Finish:** Give the sauce a quick re-stir (cornstarch settles), then pour it into the pan with the vegetables. Bring to a simmer and cook for 1–2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly and turns glossy. Return all the chicken to the pan and toss to coat evenly. Cook 30 seconds more to heat through.

7. **Serve** immediately over **hot cooked white rice**.

## CHANGES MADE

1. **Sauce instructions expanded:** Original step 2 just said "mix sauce ingredients" without listing them — now all sauce ingredients and quantities are explicitly named, and includes a note to dissolve the cornstarch fully.
2. **Cornstarch re-stir reminder added:** Cornstarch settles out of liquid; added a note to re-stir before pouring into the pan.
3. **Cooking times clarified:** Changed vague "about 5 minutes" to specific flip timing (2–3 min per side) for better crispiness guidance.
4. **Excess oil management:** Added instruction to pour off most oil before sautéing veggies — ⅓ cup is a lot of residual oil for vegetables.
5. **Crowding warning added:** Explicit tip about not overcrowding, which is the #1 mistake with this type of recipe.
6. **Internal temperature noted:** Added 165°F/74°C doneness reference for food safety with chicken.
7. **Organized ingredients into logical groups** (marinade / sauce / stir-fry) to reduce confusion between the two sets of soy sauce, oyster sauce, cornstarch, and black pepper.
8. **Shake off excess cornstarch:** Added this step to prevent gummy coating and burnt starch in the oil.
