### Chicken Tortilla Soup

**Ingredients:**
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
- 1 (15 oz) can black beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 (15 oz) can fire-roasted diced tomatoes
- 1 (15 oz) can corn, drained (or 1½ cups frozen corn)
- 4 cups chicken broth
- 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- ½ teaspoon garlic powder
- ½ teaspoon onion powder
- ½ teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
- ¼ teaspoon black pepper
- Juice of 1 lime

**Toppings:**
- Tortilla chips or fried tortilla strips
- Avocado, diced
- Sour cream
- Shredded cheese (Monterey Jack or Mexican blend)
- Fresh cilantro
- Lime wedges

**Instructions:**

1. **Sauté aromatics:** Heat **1 tablespoon olive oil** in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add **diced onion** and cook 3–4 minutes until softened. Add **3 cloves minced garlic** and **minced jalapeño**; cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
2. **Build the soup:** Add **chicken broth (4 cups)**, **diced tomatoes (1 can)**, **black beans (1 can)**, **corn (1 can)**, **cumin (1 tsp)**, **chili powder (1 tsp)**, **garlic powder (½ tsp)**, **onion powder (½ tsp)**, **salt (½ tsp)**, and **pepper (¼ tsp)**. Stir to combine.
3. **Cook the chicken:** Nestle **1 lb chicken breasts** into the liquid. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer **20–25 minutes** until chicken reaches an internal temperature of **165°F (74°C)**.
4. **Shred and finish:** Remove chicken, shred with two forks, and return to the pot. Stir in **juice of 1 lime**. Taste and adjust seasoning.
5. **Serve:** Ladle into bowls and top with tortilla chips/strips, avocado, sour cream, shredded cheese, cilantro, and lime wedges.

**⚠️ Safety & Tips:**
- **Chicken temp:** Always verify 165°F with an instant-read thermometer before shredding.
- **Jalapeño:** Wear gloves or wash hands immediately after handling; seeds and ribs carry the most heat.
- **Tortilla strips:** If frying your own, oil should be 350°F; stand back to avoid splatter.
- **Common mistake:** Don't boil the soup vigorously with the chicken in it — a gentle simmer keeps the meat tender.
